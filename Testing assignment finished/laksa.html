Laksa

Laksa is another iconic dish in Malaysia and across Southeast Asia, famous for its rich and spicy noodle soup.

The appeal of laksa lies in its bold flavors—whether it’s the creamy richness of curry laksa or the refreshing tang of asam laksa.

For many Malaysians, laksa is not just comfort food but also a dish that showcases the diverse local tastes and traditions.

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. Season the chicken thighs with salt and pepper (we used white pepper, but you can also use black pepper), and place on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  3. Bake in the oven for 40 minutes. Remove from the oven and set aside.
  4. While the chicken is cooking, heat 2 tablespoons vegetable oil in a large saucepan or medium pot over medium heat.
  5. Add the minced garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute.
  6. Throw in the minced lemongrass and chilies, and cook for 3 minutes.
  7. Add the laksa paste and brown sugar.
  8. Fry for another 3 minutes, letting all the flavors meld together.
  9. Add the chicken stock, coconut milk, and fish sauce. Bring to a boil.
  10. Add the soy puffs, cover, and allow to simmer for 10 minutes.
  11. Pour the broth over the noodles and chicken, and add a couple pieces of soy puff to each.
  12. Top with the cooked shrimp, bean sprouts, cilantro, and fried shallots. Serve with extra lime wedges
