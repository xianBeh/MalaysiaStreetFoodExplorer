Char Kway Teow

Char Kway Teow is one of Malaysia’s most famous street foods, especially popular in Penang.

The name means “stir-fried flat rice noodles” in Hokkien and Teochew dialects, and it’s known for its smoky aroma, often referred to as wok hei (the “breath of the wok”).

A key feature of authentic Char Kway Teow is that it’s cooked quickly in small portions over a flaming wok, giving the noodles their signature charred and smoky taste.

Recipe

  1. Soak the dried noodles in warm water for 30 to 45 minutes.
  2. Transfer to a colander and let the excess water drain.
  3. Add 2 teaspoons dark soy sauce, 2 tablespoons regular soy sauce, 1 tablespoon fish sauce, 1 teaspoon shrimp sauce or shrimp paste, 1 tablespoon oyster sauce, ⅛ teaspoon ground white pepper, and 1 teaspoon sugar in a small bowl.
  4. Mix until combined, and set aside.
  5. Heat your wok to medium heat, and spread 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil around the perimeter of your wok.
  6. Add the sliced Chinese sausages and stir-fry for 20 seconds. Add the garlic along with the shrimps and fish tofu.
  7. Continue stir-frying for another 20 seconds.
  8. Now, turn the wok to high heat.
  9. Spread 1 tablespoon shaoxing wine around the perimeter of the wok.
  10. Stir-fry for another 15 seconds. Add the noodles.
  11. Next add the mung bean sprouts and gently mix everything together for 1 minute.
