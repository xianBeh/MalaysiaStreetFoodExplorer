Rojak

Rojak is a unique and flavorful Malaysian dish that reflects the country’s multicultural food heritage.

The word rojak itself means “mixture” in Malay, which perfectly describes the dish—a delicious blend of sweet, sour, spicy, and savory flavors all in one.

The magic of rojak lies in the balance of contrasting flavors and textures: crunchy fruits or fritters combined with rich, tangy, and spicy sauces.

Recipe

  1. Remove skin from jicama, mangoes and pineapple if necessary.
  2. Cut all fruit and vegetable ingredients roughly into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Place into a bowl and set aside in the fridge.
  4. Mix all all sauce ingredients into a bowl until even.
  5. Transfer sauce into a cooking pot and cook on low to medium heat for 20-22 minutes, stirring often, until the sauce thickens to a glue-like consistency.
  6. Remove pot from heat and allow the sauce to cool for 10 minutes.
  7. Bring out the salad bowl from the fridge and add sauce. Stir evenly.
  8. Top with peanuts and sesame seeds.
