Nasi Lemak

Iconic Dishes: Nasi Lemak, the Quintessential Malay Breakfast

Nasi lemak is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes in Malaysia, often considered the country’s national dish.

The name literally means “rich rice” in Malay, referring to the creamy richness of the rice that’s cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves

Traditionally, nasi lemak is served with sambal, ikan bilis, kacang, hard-boiled egg and slices of cucumber.

Recipe

  1. Blend ingredient A until smooth and set aside.
  2. Put in rice, water, salt, coconut milk, knotted pandan leaves and AJI-NO-MOTO® into a rice cooker.
  3. Heat oil and sauté blended ingredients A until oil rises to the top.
  4. Pour in tamarind juice and stir well.
  5. Add in salt, sugar and AJI-NO-MOTO®.
  6. Let it cook for 15 minutes.
  7. Serve hot with sambal, hard boiled egg, cucumber, peanut and fried anchovy.
