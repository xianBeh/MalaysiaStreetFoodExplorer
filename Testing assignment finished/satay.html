Satay

Satay is a popular Southeast Asian dish, especially well-loved in Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, and Singapore.

It’s a street food favorite that has also become a must-have at gatherings, night markets, and festive occasions.

The combination of smoky grilled meat and creamy peanut sauce makes satay irresistible.

Beyond being delicious, satay also reflects the region’s food culture—communal, flavorful, and made for sharing.

Recipe

  1. Coarsely chop the garlic, onion and lemongrass.
  2. Finely grate the galangal and turmeric root.
  3. Blend all the ingredients (but the chicken) in a blender.
  4. Marinate the meat with the spice mixture and refrigerate for at least 4 hours.
  5. Before skewering the meat, soak the wooden skewers in cold water for an hour (or use metal skewers).
  6. Brush the meat with oil on both sides. Alternatively to using a brush, you can use a piece of crushed lemongrass.
  7. Then grill the meat over high heat for 5 minutes on each side.
  8. Serve together with peanut sauce, rice and cubes of cucumber.
